Privacy Policy

Effective Date: [Insert Date]

  • 1. Introduction
    Welcome to AMET University. We are committed to protecting the privacy and security of our students, faculty, staff, and visitors. This Privacy Policy outlines how we collect, use, and safeguard your personal information when you interact with our website, services, and activities related to AMET University.
  • 2. Information We Collect
    • Personal Information: Name, contact details, date of birth, identification documents, educational background, and other details provided during admissions, registration, and student enrollment.
    • Academic & Professional Information: Course enrollments, grades, academic records, research contributions, and employment history.
    • Technical Data: IP addresses, device information, browsing history on our website, and cookies for enhancing user experience.
    • Financial Information: Tuition fees, payment details, and scholarship-related data.
    • Health & Safety Information: Medical records required for hostel stays, onboard training, or emergency purposes.
    • Event Participation Data: Information related to student participation in Marine Traffic events, cultural activities, and industry summits.
  • 3. How We Use Your Information
    • Processing admissions, academic services, and student affairs.
    • Facilitating industry placements and career guidance.
    • Managing campus infrastructure, security, and student welfare.
    • Enhancing website functionality and improving user experience.
    • Conducting research, surveys, and statistical analysis.
    • Complying with regulatory and accreditation requirements.
  • 4. Sharing & Disclosure of Information
    • Educational and Regulatory Bodies: UGC, AICTE, DG Shipping, and international accreditation organizations.
    • Placement & Industry Partners: Companies recruiting AMET students and maritime industry collaborators.
    • Third-Party Service Providers: IT support, payment processors, and event management teams.
    • Legal Authorities: When required by law or to protect university policies and legal obligations.
  • 5. Data Security
    We take appropriate security measures to protect your personal data from unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. Security protocols include encryption, firewalls, and restricted access to sensitive information.
  • 6. Your Rights & Choices
    • Access and review the personal data we hold about you.
    • Request corrections or updates to inaccurate information.
    • Withdraw consent for non-essential data processing.
    • Request deletion of your personal data (subject to legal and institutional obligations).
    • Opt-out of marketing and promotional communications.
  • 7. Cookies & Tracking Technologies
    Our website uses cookies to enhance user experience, analyze site traffic, and personalize content. You can manage or disable cookies through your browser settings.
  • 8. Data Retention
    We retain your personal data for as long as necessary to fulfill academic, legal, and institutional obligations. Upon graduation or discontinuation, some records may be archived for alumni services and regulatory compliance.
  • 9. International Students & Global Collaborations
    For international students and partnerships, we comply with relevant data protection laws, including GDPR and applicable maritime education regulations.
  • 10. Changes to This Privacy Policy
    We may update this Privacy Policy periodically. Any significant changes will be communicated via our website and official university communications.
